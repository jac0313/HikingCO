Tips to Safely Hike in Colorado

Be aware of altitude sickness. It is much harder to breathe at higher because the atmospheric pressure and oxygen percentage decrease at a rapid rate.

Colorado has a dry climate and it is easy to become dehydrated. A good rule of thumb is to drink one liter of water every two hours while hiking.

Be prepared for the weather to change quickly. Afternoon storms in the summer are common and can bring heavy rain, lightning and hail. Hiking early is best.

It's likely that you will encounter wildlife on your hike. Please remember these animals are wild and should not be approached. Stay calm, make your presence known, and keep your distance.

The sun in Colorado is intense. Colorado's thin atmosphere allows for greater penetration of solar radiation and it can mean really bad sun burns and be harsh on the eyes. Wear sunglasses and plenty of sunscreen, at least 30spf.

Hikers of all skill levels know to prepare for the unexpected. Bring emergency supplies and extra layers of clothing to keep you and your party safe in all circumstances.
